Strategic Implementation Framework: Leveraging Agentic AI and Multi-Dimensional Intelligence for the CGI Business Analytics Competition
1. Executive Strategy and The Higher Education Crisis Context
1.1 The "Data2Diamonds" Philosophy in Competitive Analytics
Success in the CGI Business Analytics Competition necessitates a fundamental realignment of the analytical approach, moving beyond mere data presentation to the extraction of actionable, high-value business intelligence. This strategic pivot aligns with CGI’s corporate methodology known as "Data2Diamonds," which posits that raw data is merely the geological substrate from which "diamonds"—transformative business insights—must be mined through rigorous processing and contextualization.1 In the high-stakes environment of this competition, the winning submission cannot simply describe the state of higher education; it must demonstrate the capacity to alter that state through strategic intervention. The rubric’s emphasis on Storytelling, Analytical Depth, Business Value, UX, and Innovation demands a solution that traverses the full maturity curve of analytics: from Descriptive (what is happening) to Predictive (what will happen) and, crucially, to Prescriptive (what should be done).3
The common pitfall in such competitions is a disproportionate focus on the Descriptive tier—building elaborate dashboards that effectively function as "rear-view mirrors." While understanding historical trends is necessary, it is insufficient for victory. The "Diamond" in this context is the Prescriptive layer, where mathematical optimization and algorithmic decision-making provide university administrators with a concrete roadmap for resource allocation.1 By adopting the "Client Proximity" model, a core tenet of CGI’s operational philosophy, the analysis must simulate a partnership with a vulnerable institution, addressing its specific existential threats with the same rigor one would apply to a Fortune 500 client.4 This approach ensures that the "Business Value" criterion is met not by abstract theorizing, but by simulating a deployable, revenue-generating solution.
1.2 The Narrative Arc: The "Demographic Cliff" of 2026
The most potent narrative available to anchor this analysis—and one that provides the requisite urgency for a winning business case—is the impending "Demographic Cliff." Beginning in 2026, the United States higher education sector will confront a precipitous and structural decline in the number of traditional high school graduates, a delayed demographic echo of the birth rate decline precipitated by the 2008 financial crisis.5 This is not a cyclical downturn but a permanent contraction of the addressable market for traditional four-year degrees.
This framework positions the analytics solution as a "Strategic Resilience Engine" specifically engineered for the "Murky Middle" of the market—regional public universities and small private non-profit colleges that lack the endowments of elite institutions and the scale of mega-universities.7 Unlike Harvard or Yale, which are insulated by massive brand equity and financial reserves, these mid-tier institutions face a binary outcome: adapt through data-driven precision or face closure. The narrative arc of the submission will argue that survival in the post-2026 landscape requires a transition from intuition-based enrollment management to "Precision Education Intelligence."
The solution addresses this existential risk through three strategic pillars:
1. Revenue Stabilization: Using predictive modeling to identify "Yield" risks—admitted students who are unlikely to enroll without intervention.8
2. Retention Optimization: deploying machine learning to flag enrolled students at risk of attrition before they drop out, thereby preserving tuition revenue and improving outcomes.9
3. Financial Efficiency: Utilizing linear programming to optimize the allocation of financial aid, ensuring that every dollar spent maximizes the probability of enrollment and student success.10
This narrative scaffolding transforms the project from a technical exercise into a strategic imperative, directly satisfying the "Storytelling" criterion by connecting data points to a coherent, high-stakes business reality.
1.3 The Agentic Advantage: Paradigm Shift in Development
The "Innovation" score in the competition will be driven not just by what is built, but how it is built. The constraints of a single-day competition usually limit the complexity of the output. However, the utilization of Google Antigravity, an agent-first Integrated Development Environment (IDE), allows a small team to amplify its output capabilities by an order of magnitude.1 By functioning as "AI Architects" rather than mere coders, the team can offload the scaffolding, boilerplate generation, and routine data cleaning tasks to autonomous agents, reserving human intellect for high-level architectural decisions and narrative refinement.
This workflow leverages the concept of "Agent Skills"—custom-defined, reusable instruction sets that extend the AI's capabilities.1 Instead of repeatedly prompting the AI to "use the correct API fields," the team will engineer a scorecard-api-expert skill that permanently encodes the complex taxonomy of the College Scorecard dataset. Similarly, a cgi-brand-stylist skill will ensure that every visual element generated by the agent adheres strictly to CGI’s brand identity (specifically the use of CGI Red, Hex #E31937).11 This demonstrates a level of technical sophistication that signals to the judges that the team is operating at the bleeding edge of software engineering, effectively "future-proofing" the solution just as the analysis seeks to future-proof the client institution.

2. Technical Architecture and The Agentic Workflow
2.1 Google Antigravity: Configuration and Philosophy
The shift to an agentic workflow requires a meticulous setup of the development environment. Google Antigravity differs from traditional IDEs like VS Code by integrating a Large Language Model (LLM) deeply into the file system, terminal, and browser, allowing it to "act" rather than just "suggest".1
Step-by-Step Environment Initialization: To ensure a conflict-free workspace, the team must launch Google Antigravity and select the "Start fresh" option during the onboarding wizard. Importing settings from previous IDEs can introduce "lint" or legacy configurations that confuse the agent's reasoning capabilities.1
Operational Mode Configuration:
The agent's autonomy settings are the control rods of this reactor. For a competition setting, speed is paramount, but precision is non-negotiable.
* Operational Mode: Select "Agent-assisted development." This mode keeps the human in the loop for high-level plan approval while delegating the actual code writing and file manipulation to the agent. It strikes the necessary balance between control and velocity.1
* Terminal Execution: Set to "Auto." This is a critical accelerator. It empowers the agent to execute shell commands—such as pip install pandas scikit-learn pulp or npm run dev—without pausing for human confirmation on every step. This allows the human operator to focus on the strategic roadmap while the agent handles the "grunt work" of dependency management in the background.1
* Review Policy: Set to "Agent decides." In this configuration, the AI will only request human intervention for potentially destructive actions (like deleting non-empty directories) or when it detects ambiguity in the instructions. For 95% of tasks, it will proceed autonomously.
Workspace Architecture:
The team should initialize a new directory named cgi-analytics-2026. This will serve as the monorepo root, containing both the Next.js frontend (for the dashboard) and the Python FastAPI backend (for the predictive and prescriptive engines). The agent should be instructed to structure the project logically, separating src/components for UI elements, src/lib for utility functions, and backend/models for the serialized machine learning artifacts.

3. Data Foundation: The Architecture of Intelligence
3.1 The College Scorecard Ecosystem and Cohort Strategy
The foundation of this analysis is the U.S. Department of Education's College Scorecard dataset. However, "using the data" is not enough; one must understand the provenance and limitations of the data to derive valid insights. A sophisticated analysis acknowledges the "Cohort Lag." Data released in the 2023-24 academic year typically reflects earnings for students who graduated four years prior.23
The framework explicitly selects the 4-year post-completion earnings (latest.earnings.4_yrs_after_completion.median) as the primary ROI metric. This is a deliberate deviation from older analyses that used "10 years after entry." The Department of Education has recently highlighted data quality issues with the entry-cohort variables and has shifted focus to exit-cohorts (completers) to provide a more accurate reflection of the value of the degree.17 By strictly using the post-completion metric, the analysis avoids the confounding variable of non-completers dragging down earnings averages, thus providing a fairer assessment of the institution's educational value.
3.2 Target Variable Taxonomy
To support the "Enrollment Cliff" narrative, the team must instruct the agent to fetch a specific subset of the 2,000+ available variables. This targeted extraction prevents data bloat and focuses the analysis on financial health and student success.


Business Concept
	API Field Name (Developer-Friendly)
	Strategic Rationale
	ROI / Outcomes
	latest.earnings.4_yrs_after_completion.median
	The Gold Standard: Represents early-career stability. We explicitly avoid 10_yrs_after_entry due to recent data errata.17
	Student Debt
	latest.aid.median_debt.completers.overall
	Focuses on the debt load of graduates. High debt for dropouts is a different problem; high debt for graduates impacts the value proposition.20
	Real Affordability

	"Sticker price" is irrelevant for the target demographic. The net price for the lowest income bracket is the true measure of an institution's accessibility mission.19
	Market Demand

	A proxy for brand strength and exclusivity. High admission rates in the face of demographic decline signal extreme vulnerability.
	Student Success

	Retention

	The primary target for the Predictive Analytics model. Retention is cheaper than recruitment.21
	Demographics

	3.3 Advanced Feature Engineering: Mining the Diamonds
Raw data is rarely sufficient for high-performance machine learning models. The team must instruct the agent to engineer "Diamond Features"—derived metrics that reveal hidden structural truths about the institutions.
1. The "Value-Add" Efficiency Ratio:
  

         * Strategic Insight: This metric identifies "Social Mobility Engines"—schools that take low-income students and launch them into the middle class efficiently. A high ratio indicates a "Diamond" institution that should be benchmarked against.
2. The "Resilience Risk Index":
This composite score aggregates vulnerability factors into a single KPI for the dashboard.
         * Formula Logic: Weighted sum of normalized Inverse Admission Rate (Selectivity), Federal Loan Rate, and Completion Rate.
  
         * Interpretation: High admission rates + high reliance on loans + low completion rates = High Risk of insolvency when the demographic cliff hits.
3. Geographic Isolation Score: Using the location.lat and location.lon fields, the agent should calculate the Haversine distance to the nearest Combined Statistical Area (CSA) with a population over 500,000.26
            * Hypothesis: Rural institutions are at significantly higher risk from the demographic cliff due to the lack of a local commuter base. This geospatial feature is a critical predictor for the retention model.
3.4 Automated Data Hygiene Pipeline
The College Scorecard dataset is riddled with data quality issues, such as strings like "PrivacySuppressed" and "NULL" which will cause machine learning models to crash. The scorecard-api-expert skill must include a cleaning pipeline.
            * Handling Suppression: "Replace all instances of 'PrivacySuppressed' with NaN (Not a Number)."
            * Peer-Group Imputation: "Do not simply fill missing values with the global mean. Impute missing numeric values using the median of the specific school.carnegie_basic class.".27 This ensures that a missing graduation rate for a small liberal arts college is estimated based on other small liberal arts colleges, not research universities, preserving the structural integrity of the data.

            3. Trend Analysis (Center): The Revenue-Enrollment Divergence: A dual-axis time-series chart showing "Total Enrollment" vs. "Net Tuition Revenue" over the last 5 years.
            * Insight: If Enrollment is flat or declining but Revenue is rising, the institution is surviving solely by raising prices—a strategy that is mathematically unsustainable when the demographic cliff arrives. This chart visually demonstrates the urgency of the problem.
            4. Detail (Bottom): The Peer Comparison Engine: Utilizing a K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) algorithm running in the background, this table auto-populates 5 statistical peers based on size, control (public/private), and location.29 It benchmarks the client against these peers on Retention and Yield, grounding the analysis in competitive reality.

5. Tier 2: Predictive Analytics - The "Will"
5.1 Predicting Enrollment Yield and Retention
While Descriptive Analytics looks backward, Predictive Analytics looks forward. The "Business Value" here is financial forecasting. If a university can accurately predict which admitted students will actually enroll (Yield) or which current students are likely to drop out (Retention), they can intervene proactively.
Model Architecture:
            * Algorithm: XGBoost (eXtreme Gradient Boosting) is the algorithm of choice. Unlike linear regression, XGBoost excels at handling the non-linear relationships and missing values inherent in educational data.1 It consistently outperforms other models in tabular data competitions.
            * Target Variable: latest.student.retention_rate.four_year.full_time.
            * Features: Net_Price, Median_Debt, SAT_Average, Pell_Grant_Rate, and the engineered Geographic_Isolation_Score.
Agentic Implementation:
The team uses the agent to handle the model training pipeline:
Prompt: "Act as a Data Scientist. Train an XGBoost regressor to predict retention rates. Use the cleaned dataset. Implement 5-fold cross-validation to prevent overfitting. Optimize for Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE). Once trained, save the model artifact to backend/models/retention_xgb.json."
5.2 Explainability with SHAP (The "Why")
"Black box" models are fatal in business settings. Administrators will not trust a machine that simply says "Student X is High Risk" without explanation. To bridge this trust gap and score high on "Analytical Depth," the solution integrates SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations).
            * The Innovation: The dashboard includes a "Force Plot" for individual risk assessment.33
            * Scenario: A student is flagged as "High Risk" (Red). The SHAP plot visualizes the contribution of each feature to that prediction. It might show that "High Net Price" is pushing the risk up (+0.15), while "High SAT Math" is pushing it down (-0.05).
            * Actionable Intelligence: This tells the administrator exactly what the problem is. If the driver is financial (Price), the intervention should be a scholarship. If the driver is academic (SAT), the intervention should be tutoring. This connects the "Will" directly to the "What To Do."
5.3 Automated Fairness Audit
Before any model is deployed, it must be audited. This is where the fairness-bias-auditor skill creates value.
            * The Audit: The agent calculates the error rates of the model across different demographic groups (specifically Pell Grant recipients vs. non-recipients).
            * The Narrative: "Our initial model showed a 5% higher false negative rate for Pell-dominant schools, meaning it was failing to identify at-risk low-income students. We adjusted the sample weights to achieve Demographic Parity, ensuring our tool does not unfairly penalize institutions serving at-risk populations".1 This explicitly addresses the ethical dimension of analytics, a key differentiator in modern data science competitions.

6. Tier 3: Prescriptive Analytics - The "Diamond"
6.1 Financial Aid Optimization Model
This module represents the pinnacle of the Data2Diamonds maturity curve. Most teams will stop at prediction. This team will build an optimization engine that tells the university exactly how to distribute its limited scholarship budget to survive the demographic cliff.
Mathematical Formulation (Linear Programming):
The problem is framed as a variation of the "Knapsack Problem," where the goal is to pack the "knapsack" (the incoming class) with the highest possible value (enrollment yield/revenue) without breaking the constraints (budget).
            * Objective Function (  ): Maximize Total Predicted Enrollment (Yield).
  
Where    is the probability of student    enrolling given Aid amount    (derived from the Predictive Model), and    is a binary decision variable (1 if aid is offered, 0 if not).
            * Constraints:
               1. Budget Constraint: The sum of all awarded aid must not exceed the total institutional budget (  ).
  
               2. Academic Quality Constraint: The average SAT score of the enrolled class must meet or exceed a specific threshold (to maintain rankings and accreditation).
               3. Equity Constraint: To ensure diversity, a minimum percentage of the total aid budget must be allocated to Pell-eligible students.1
6.2 Implementation with PuLP and Python
To implement this, the team utilizes the PuLP library in Python, the industry standard for linear optimization.1
Code Logic (Agent Instructions):
The agent is tasked with writing a Python script that:
                  1. Initializes an LpProblem with LpMaximize.
                  2. Defines LpVariable dictionaries for each student/aid combination.
                  3. Uses lpSum to construct the Budget and Equity constraints.
                  4. Solves the problem and outputs a CSV list of students who should receive offers.
The integration of PuLP demonstrates that the team can move beyond statistical correlation (ML) to mathematical certainty (Optimization), a distinction that defines the "Prescriptive" tier.
6.3 The "Scenario Planner" Interface
To satisfy the "UX" criterion, this complex mathematics must be hidden behind a simple, intuitive interface—the "Scenario Planner".37
                  * The Controls: The dashboard features sliders for "Total Budget" ($5M - $10M) and "Target Average SAT" (1000 - 1400).
                  * Real-Time Feedback: As the user moves the budget slider down (simulating a funding cut), the "Predicted Class Size" gauge instantly drops. This provides immediate, visceral feedback on the trade-offs.
                  * Business Impact: This tool empowers a University President to make data-driven decisions in real-time. “If we cut the financial aid budget by 10%, we lose 50 students and our average SAT score drops by 20 points.” This is high-level strategic intelligence that justifies the investment in the analytics platform.
